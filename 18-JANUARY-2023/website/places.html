Almora Hill Station

Almora, a beautiful district in the Kumaon region of Uttarakhand in India, enthralls tourists worldwide with a panoramic view of the Himalayas. Almora is a very beautiful city in the state of Uttarakhand, India. Pithoragarh and Champawat are situated in the east, Pauri in the west, Bageshwar in the north, and Nainital in the south. Almora is not only famous for its rich cultural heritage, unique handicrafts, sumptuous food, and splendid wildlife, but the beautiful Himalayan mountain ranges are an unmatched amount of natural beauty. In these hills, nature’s hospitality can be received by all human beings at any time. The enchanting beauties of the Himalayas, their expansive climate, and soothing green envelopes leave you nothing more to be desired. Known for its cultural diversity, Almora is a beautiful hill station situated at an average altitude of 1,638 meters above sea level and spread over a 5-kilometer-long ridge on Kashyap hill. Surrounded by dwindling pines and old oaks, the city has a divine natural aura that is enough to tempt anyone and everyone. To make the scenery even more melodious, the snow-capped Himalayas stand with utmost magnificence to delight the eyes of their adoring ones. Almora is famous for its rich cultural heritage, unique handicrafts, and delicious cuisine, thus it is also known as the cultural capital of Kumaon. It is also the best hill station for honeymoon for newly married couples. The church of Almora is also somewhere old. This church is of the British era, this church works as a charm in the beauty of this city. Bal Mithai of Almora is famous not only in the country but also abroad. Apart from Bal Mithai, other sweets here are also very tasty and good. Almora is also the main center of education. Almora is the main center of education in Kumaon. Children from distant mountains come here to study. This tourist place is connected by road to the entire Kumaon and Uttarakhand state. Because of this people can easily reach here. The largest campus of Kumaun University is located in Almora city itself.

Munsiyari - The Little Kashmir

Munsiyari - The Little Kashmir Munsiyari is a comely hamlet situated in the Pithoragarh district of Uttarakhand state, nestled in the snow capped peaks of the Himalayas Munsiyari offers splendid vistas of natural beauteousness. This scenic hill station is perched at an altitude of 2298mts above sea level. Munsiyari literally translated means place with snow, and is also referred as Little Kashmir.

Dharchula

Dharchula is a town in Pithoragarh district in the northern state of Uttarakhand, India, situated at an elevation of 940 m above sea level, surrounded by peaks from all sides and Kali river cutting through the middle, dividing the area into two towns on either bank of the river - one in India and the other in Nepal. River Kali originates from Kalapani and forms the border between India and Nepal. People of the two towns have similar traditions, culture, and lifestyle, and can move across the border without a passport or visa. The area has a mixture of Kumaouni and Rung language, traditions and culture. They move freely across the river as Indians and Nepalis do not need passport or visa to cross the soft border. Dharchula lies about 92 km (57 mi) north of Pithoragarh town, along the route of the Kailash-Mansarovar pilgrimage tour.
